[[back to contents|contentsadulthood]]So, what does //On Vanity and the Art of Comparison// tell us? Perhaps that, if you observe, as all humans do, the people around you, you are bound to feel lesser - less beautiful, less feminine, less worthy. I expected to come to some bright, life-changing idea at the end of this exploration, but I didn't. Perhaps you have. Everybody is always trying to stop caring what everybody else thinks, to love ourselves. I think in order to stop caring, you have to eradicate all negative thoughts of yourself, because then, you won't even become aware of the negative things people could be thinking about you, which they probably aren't. I gave away a lot of my clothes the first and only time I fell in love. I realised that a lot of the things I bought and wore were not things I chose for myself, but instead based on assumptions of what other people had expected me to wear. The coat I bought after that house-party isn't me. The red shoes I bought, because red is sexy and cool, are not me. I can be beautiful without possession definining beauty for me. I want to be a lot of things - more empathetic, quieter, calmer, less depressed, better at public speaking - but I feel that this also enters into the realm of vanity and comparison. It's not a physical type of vanity, but one assigned to that of personality. If I want to be these things, is it only to save myself from judgement of others? There is no simple answer that I know of yet, to resist the shine of beauty and grace, but I am young still. I have time to figure it out.
